## Q1: Has the Whittaker diagram persisted in contemporary textbooks?

**Registered**: 2026-05-15 (Kim)

**Question**: Do contemporary biology textbooks include a
Whittaker diagram in the ecology section? Kim's own texts
are old; a longevity check on whether Whittaker's idea has
stuck for 50 years.

**Preliminary finding (web search, 2026-05-15)**: Yes — the
diagram remains a standard inclusion in contemporary ecology
and introductory biology textbooks.

- **Campbell Biology** (Pearson; the dominant US intro-bio
  text through multiple editions) covers Whittaker's
  climate-based biome classification in its ecology unit
  (Chapter 52 in earlier editions; equivalent chapter in
  current editions).
- **Ricklefs, *The Economy of Nature*** uses the Whittaker
  diagram as canonical Figure 5.5 — this is the figure
  plotbiomes (and therefore whittakerr) vendors. Ricklefs's
  textbook continues to be published.
- **Teaching modules** built around the diagram are in
  active use, including the Carleton SERC EDDIE module
  "Biomes, Vegetation Structure, and Canopy Height" and
  the Wyoming Biodiversity Database's "Interpreting
  Whittaker Biome Diagrams."
- **Current research literature** continues to use the
  diagram as a framing device — 2024 papers on global
  tree mortality, photo-point distributions, and biome
  classification map their data against Whittaker
  polygons.

**A wrinkle worth noting**: "Whittaker-like" variants now
circulate alongside the original. Different textbooks and
sources have modified the polygons — adjusting boundaries,
adding biomes, splitting categories. The diagram is now
plural rather than singular. The wildplantspost blog
("A new Whittaker biome diagram") and various textbook
variations are examples. This is itself a teachable point
in the eventual document — the original endures, but it
has spawned a family of derivatives, which is what happens
to influential figures over time.

**What would resolve it more fully**: a small survey of
~5 currently-used college biology and ecology textbooks
(latest editions of Campbell, Sadava, Mader, Krebs, Begon
et al., Cain et al.) with a yes/no on whether the original
Whittaker diagram appears, plus any modifications used. A
half-day's work in a university library or via publisher
sample-chapter PDFs.

**Not urgent**. Kim flagged it as "not tremendously
important." Captured here so it stays a real, addressable
question rather than dissolving into wishful prose in the
History chapter.

## How to add questions

When a question surfaces during a working session that we
can't immediately resolve and don't want to lose:

1. Add a numbered entry above with the date and a clear
   question statement.
2. Capture any preliminary findings (a quick search, a
   sentence of recollection, a hypothesis).
3. Note what would resolve it more fully — what work or
   what data is needed.
4. Flag urgency honestly (most questions are not urgent).

Resolved questions can be moved to a "Resolved" section
below as the file grows, or removed if the resolution is
fully captured in the document or a project file.
